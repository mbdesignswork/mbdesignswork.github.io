FAQ

Common questions before starting.

A quick overview of what is included, what is separate, and how custom prototype work is handled.

Do I need a finished schematic or PCB before contacting you?

No. You can start with an idea, sketch, photos, notes, rough concept, or prototype problem. If the project is still early, the Hardware Idea Review is usually the best first step.

Why are the starting prices reasonable?

Bradshaw Hardware Labs is built for practical, budget-conscious prototype support. The goal is to focus on useful engineering work, clear next steps, and low overhead instead of bloated agency-style pricing.

Can you design the full PCB for me?

Yes, simple low-voltage PCB layout can be quoted after the schematic and scope are clear. Dense boards, RF, high-current, complex safety-critical work, or advanced designs may not be accepted.

Are parts, PCB manufacturing, or 3D printing included?

Not by default. Service pricing covers design and support labor only. Components, PCB manufacturing, 3D print material, shipping, outside fabrication, specialty modules, and replacement parts are separate unless clearly included in a written quote.

Can you repair my physical prototype?

Physical hands-on testing, soldering, rework, or repair may be quoted separately if the project is local or shipped. The Prototype Debug Plan itself provides a troubleshooting plan, not a guaranteed repair.

Do you work on mains voltage or house wiring?

No. Work is limited to low-voltage electronics, DC systems up to 48 VDC, and isolated low-voltage AC or signal circuits. No mains wiring, building electrical work, breaker panels, life-safety systems, medical certification, or licensed engineering sign-off.

What should I send for a project request?

Send a short project description, your goal, photos, sketches, schematic or PCB files if available, symptoms, measurements, budget range, and timeline. The more context you provide, the easier it is to recommend the right next step.
