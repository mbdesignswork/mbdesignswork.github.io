Rates & billing

How starter payments and extra hours work.

Main project rate: CA$65/hr. Hourly starter payments cover the 2-hour minimum, so follow-up invoices only charge total hours worked minus the 2 hours already paid. Electronics & Prototype Consultation is CA$150, schematic/PCB review starts at CA$150, and design, debug, CAD, firmware, and recreation starter services begin with the 2-hour minimum.

For hourly starter services, the upfront payment covers the first 2 hours. If a project takes more time, the follow-up invoice is based on the total hours worked minus the 2 hours already paid. Larger prototype phases can still be quoted once the work is clear.

Pricing covers design and support labor only. Components, PCB manufacturing, 3D print material, print time, outside fabrication, shipping, specialty modules, and replacement parts are separate unless clearly included in a written quote.

FAQ

Common questions before starting.

Straight answers about projects, hourly work, consultation, parts, CAD, 3D printing, firmware, and deliverables.

Who is behind MB Designs Work?

MB Designs Work is run by Michael Bradshaw, a hands-on hardware builder with a McMaster Electrical Engineering & Management background, electronics manufacturing support experience, and practical experience in PCB work, testing, debugging, CAD fixtures, 3D printing, and prototype support.

What kinds of projects do you take on?

Projects include consumer electronics, product prototypes, toy circuits, custom boards, sensor circuits, audio circuits, Bluetooth/IoT prototypes, motor-control hardware, breadboard-to-PCB work, PCB reviews, firmware bring-up, reverse engineering, test fixtures, enclosures, brackets, and 3D printed support parts.

Why use hourly pricing instead of only fixed packages?

Prototype work changes once parts, files, measurements, and real constraints are reviewed. Hourly pricing keeps the work flexible and focused on the next useful technical step. Fixed starting points are still available for Electronics & Prototype Consultation and PCB/schematic review.

Can you design the full PCB for me?

Yes. PCB design can be handled hourly or quoted by phase after the schematic, requirements, parts, size, connectors, and complexity are clear. Dense boards, RF, high-speed, high-current, or multi-board projects are reviewed first and quoted based on the actual work.

Do you do firmware and Bluetooth?

Yes, for bring-up and practical prototype support: simple microcontroller logic, sensor interfaces, BLE/Bluetooth modules, serial communication, motor-control logic, and hardware-firmware integration. Larger production firmware systems are reviewed first and quoted based on the actual work.

Do you offer 3D printing?

Yes. CAD and 3D printing support can include enclosures, brackets, clips, PCB holders, test fixtures, programming fixtures, small printed parts, and STEP/STL files. Design labor is hourly, while print material, print time, and outside fabrication are quoted separately.

Can you reverse engineer an old board or circuit?

Yes. Work can include tracing connections, documenting wiring, identifying parts where possible, recreating schematics/PCBs, and planning a replacement or updated revision.

Do you work on existing products or appliances?

Yes. Send photos and a short description of the product, board, wiring, CAD part, enclosure, fixture, prototype, or replacement-board goal. MB Designs Work will review it and recommend the best starting point.

What should I send for a project request?

Send a short project description, your goal, photos, sketches, schematic/PCB files if available, symptoms, measurements, part links, budget range, timeline, and what output you want: advice, files, CAD, prototype debug, firmware bring-up, 3D print support, or a quote.
